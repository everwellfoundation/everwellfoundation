We have a rule at Everwell. We don't give to feel good about ourselves. We give because something needs doing and we happen to be in a position to help do it.

Milo nearly broke that rule — because it is very hard to look at a golden retriever and remain entirely unsentimental.

He came to us through the Toronto Humane Society or rather, his story did. A golden retriever, cute as they are, with a tumour that was taking things from him slowly and then all at once. The kind of diagnosis that lands like a quiet sentence. The kind that, for an animal without the right support around him, often ends one way.

What struck us about Milo wasn't the tumour. It was the tail. By every account, even in the middle of all of it, Milo's tail kept wagging. There is something about an animal's refusal to be diminished by circumstances beyond its understanding that sits with you.

The Toronto Humane Society does extraordinary work not just rehoming animals, but fighting for their welfare, funding medical care that would otherwise be out of reach, and believing, fundamentally, that an animal's life has value regardless of what it costs to protect it. We believe that too.

We supported Milo's surgery. It was successful.

He is, by all reports, enormously happy. He walks with the particular confidence of a dog who has been through something hard and come out the other side, and knows it, in whatever way dogs know things. His tail still hasn't stopped.

We gave because it needed doing. We stayed because of the tail.
